RIDING LESSONS AT ADVENT FARM

About the Facility

Lessons are available at Advent Farm, which is located on Davis Rd. in Waxhaw, NC. (just a few minutes from downtown Waxhaw).

You’ll enjoy riding in a 100’ x 200’ professionally-installed sand arena, with cavaletti and jumps available. Several fields with gentle terrain for outdoor work are on site as well.

Quiet and Private Environment

Get the most out of the time and money you put into your riding lessons! Advent Farm is a small, private facility where you’ll spend your valuable lesson time focused and learning, not dodging other riders or dealing with distracting surroundings.

Horses

Most students trailer in/bring their own horses, but there are also a small number of schooled horses that can be used for lessons.

What is Centered Riding?

"Centered Riding, created by Sally Swift (1913-2009), is based on a knowledge of human and horse anatomy, balance, movement, and on understanding how the mind affects the body and how both affect the horse. It uses centering and grounding techniques from the oriental martial arts, along with body awareness, mental imagery and sports psychology. Through increasing body awareness, inhibiting old patterns, and replacing them with a more balanced, free, and coordinated use of self, both horse and rider can move more freely and comfortably, and develop their best performance."

- content from centeredriding.org.

"Since the horse’s movement is a result of a combination of his body balance and the state of his mind, both of which are deeply influenced by the rider - in many cases dependent on the rider - you carry an immense responsibility. You must use your body in such a way that you do not interfere with his balance and movement,while at the same time indicating your intentions with clarity. This is what encourages the horse to work happily. This is where Centered Riding can be of enormous help."

- excerpt from the book Centered Riding 2: Further Exploration, by Sally Swift

Sally Swift’s book Centered Riding was first published in 1985, and has been translated into fifteen languages, selling over 800,000 copies worldwide. A second book, Centered Riding 2: Further Exploration was published in 2002 and is available in nine languages, with over 100,000 copies sold worldwide.

Learn more about Centered Riding at centeredriding.org
